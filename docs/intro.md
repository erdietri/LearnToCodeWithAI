# "Why would I take this course??"

## I know, I know - you don't like coding. 
It's hard, it's boring, and you don't understand what you can build anyway.

Or maybe you *are* interested in coding for network automation, but you just don't have the time or support to make progress. 

This course was born out of gazillions (I don't know how many) of conversations at conferences and online with network engineers and IT professionals.

Specifically, it was spurred by a conversation I had with an attendee at my AI-Powered Scripting with GitHub Copilot talk at Cisco Live. The session had two slots and was packed out and had rave reviews, even earning me Distinguished Speaker despite it being both an AI *and* coding talk (the worst combo, right??).

This attendee essentially said: "Fantastic presentation, and I'm ready to get my team using an AI coding assistant... but how do I teach them to code in the first place?"

This was the first of many moments where I realized that despite the push for network engineers to learn automation, the gap in achieving this was still the width of the Grand Canyon. 

There's a lot of reasons for this, some of which you'll learn in the course. 

But this course attempts to use AI for something good -- making it way faster and easier for you to learn network automation.

Not to use it to cheat in interviews...
not to use it to build something you don't understand...

but your secret weapon for gaining this skill rapidly and building what you need in a fraction of the time.

This course attempts to teach exactly what you need to know, when you need to know it. 

You get only as much theory as is absolutely required, and not all at once.

It stays practical, and I'm constantly coming up with new ideas to make this better and more specific to network engineers.

And perhaps most important of all, it gets you using AI in a way that's actually meaningful, which, you know, might matter for your career prospects later. (If it turns out I'm wrong, you'll find me on a farm somewhere.)

Yes, I may be a stinky poo poo developer... but I know my stuff, and I want to help.

Who knows, it might not suck as bad as you thought it would. 

You might even build something you're proud of, or that impresses your boss.

Godspeed!
