# Think Like A Developer
How many coding or Python courses have you taken that taught you how to actually think like a developer, or programmatically? ...I'd be willing to bet none - which is a problem because coding is a problem-solving skill, not something to be memorized. This video breaks down why so many people struggle to learn to code and how to approach the course going forward.

## After-video reflection

This video is merely exposure therapy for what's ahead, so instead of coding exercises and an assessment guide, I recommend grabbing a pen or opening your word processor and reflecting on the following: 

1. What obstacles are currently in my way when it comes to learning to code? Think about both external obstacles (i.e. limited time, no laptop/computer) and internal obstacles (i.e. "I'll never figure this out", "I'm not a developer", "What's the point if we have AI?"). 

2. For your external obstacles, write down how you will pursue this course in spite of them. For instance, if you have limited time, maybe you dedicate 15 minutes per day to the course.

3. For your internal obstacles, we're going to use a bit of Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT). Next to each thought, write down a more balanced, realistic version of it. For example, next to "I'll never figure this out", perhaps you replace this with "This course will challenge me, but with time and practice, I can figure this out." 

4. Determine what your goal is with this course. Are you learning to code to get a new job or a promotion? Are you passionate about automating a particular problem at work? Keep this motivation close to your chest for when we approach challenges and frustrations while learning.

5. Where can you find support and accountability? Do you have a colleague or friend who can take the course with you? Is there an online group or forum you can join?
